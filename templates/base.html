CultureCase | putting research to work

CultureCase | putting research to work 3 December 2013 Tim Poole
  • Using the ‘audience exchange’ approach to help first-time audiences
    This paper outlines the use of the ‘audience exchange’ approach to audience development. This involves facilitated group dialogue between audience members who are first-time attenders who may lack confidence or familiarity with the art form they have just encountered.
  • How people acquire new tastes in music
    This study took place over 15 months via a combination of observation and life history interviews with about 30 regulars at a Honky Tonk Night Club in a large mid-western US city.
  • The positive impacts of active music participation for infants
    To understand the developmental impact of active participation in music, this study worked with two groups of six-month old infants who attended music-based sessions with teachers and their parents.
  • How learning visual art improves creativity and changes the brain
    This study investigated the impact of visual art training on young adults’ behaviour and changes in brain activity.
  • Managing dementia through object handing in museums
    The authors of this study propose that the heritage sector could have a role to play in subjective health and wellbeing for individuals with dementia.
  • Frequent attendance at cultural events associated with reduced cancer mortality
    This paper describes a long-term study looking at correlations between taking part in certain cultural activities and cancer mortality.
  • Musicians react faster than non-musicians
    This study aimed to investigate whether long-term musical training improves unisensory (audio or tactile) and multisensory (audio and tactile) processing capacities.

Welcome to CultureCase

A resource for the cultural sector

CultureCase comprises a vast array of carefully selected academic research from universities and scholars around the world that can add value to the work of the cultural sector.

The research on the site has been edited into easily accessible summaries of key findings and insights to help inform the sector and provide the empirical evidence to support activities across arts and culture. We’ve created short accessible summaries of academic research that demonstrate the impacts of arts and culture, as well as highlighting a range of insights to help inform future planning. You can use CultureCase to help:

  • advocate for the value of culture
  • apply for funding
  • inform programming, marketing and strategic decision-making

In collating research for this resource we have selected studies from peer reviewed academic journals that respond to the challenges set by a network of cultural sector partners. You don’t need to be an expert in research to use this site: simply use the search bar, research categories or contents page to explore what’s here.

CultureCase is an experiment in communicating academic research. We want the site to evolve with your participation so please get in touch if you have a suggestion for how it might be improved or any additional academic studies you think should be included.

CultureCase will soon also include an additional data portal to provide the sector with access to high-quality data to support decision-making, planning, advocacy and artistic practice. This ‘sister site’ is being developed in collaboration with the Department of Informatics at King’s College London and will be launched later in 2018.
